Defining a safety-driven product from ambiguity

TL;DR
Type
Discovery UX
Client
Autoliv

Autoliv is a global leader in automotive safety systems, known for developing airbags, seatbelts, and advanced safety technologies. I was brought in to lead an 8-week discovery phase to define a new mobile product, turning an open brief with no defined users, goal, or purpose into a focused, meaningful product direction.

8-week discovery phase turning an undefined app brief into a differentiated, safety-focused product concept.

Challenge

The brief was simple on the surface, but complex in reality. The client wanted to be present in the app landscape, even though the reason to be was not clear. The project was also delivered by a small team, meaning most of the discovery, research, and ideation work sat with me.

Core challenges

  • No defined user problem or product goal
  • Diverging stakeholder expectations and ideas
  • A saturated and undifferentiated market
  • Uncertainty around how a digital product connects to physical safety systems
════════════════════════════════════
My Role

UX Lead — Discovery & Concept Direction. I led the discovery phase end-to-end, shaping both the process and the outcome.

Responsibilities

  • Led research across stakeholders and users
  • Drove synthesis and opportunity framing
  • Defined product direction and concept
  • Identified MVP and future roadmap
  • Presented outcomes and secured continuation
════════════════════════════════════
Approach

The project started without a clear problem or purpose, so the first step was to create alignment. Through stakeholder workshops and a Value Proposition Canvas, I helped the team converge on target users, core problems to solve, and a meaningful value proposition This helped establishing a shared foundation for everything that followed.

Research and opportunity framing

I combined competitive analysis with extensive user interviews across Europe, the US, and Asia to understand both the market and real user needs. The market was highly saturated, existing solutions lacked differentiation, and riders had clear unmet needs around safety and learning. Using opportunity mapping, I translated these insights into structured problem spaces and prioritised opportunities.

Defining the product direction

The work led to a shift from a generic app concept to a safety-focused companion, centred on education and behaviour. This direction differentiated Autoliv in a crowded space, aligned with their core mission, and aims at creating long-term user value beyond mere features.

Feasibility and validation

I worked closely with internal tech teams and an external motorbike coach and developer to explore available data, APIs, and integrations, ensuring the concept was technically viable. Early ideas were tested through scenarios and storyboards, helping validate interest and refine the direction before moving forward.

MVP and roadmap

Together with our PO I defined a focused MVP and a phased roadmap, balancing user value, technical feasibility, and business potential.

════════════════════════════════════
Outcome

The discovery phase established a clear and validated product direction, transforming an initially broad and undefined challenge into a focused and actionable vision. The work was well received across the organisation and created strong internal momentum, leading directly to continued collaboration in the following definition phase. The concept was ultimately taken forward by Autoliv’s internal team, building on the foundations we had defined.
