Simplifying a complex booking experience

TL;DR
Type
Discovery UX
Client
Irish Ferries
Year
2025

Irish Ferries is a leading ferry operator connecting Ireland, the UK, and continental Europe. Despite a strong market presence, their digital experience had not kept up with user expectations: the app was difficult to use, especially when booking, leading to confusion, errors, and a high number of customer support calls. The project focused on an 8-week definition phase to create a clear product direction before moving into design and development.

8-week definition phase for Irish Ferries,turning a fragmented, unclear brief into a shared product direction and UX foundation ready for redesign.

Challenge

The project started with a broad and somewhat unclear problem: the app was not performing well, but there was no shared understanding of what to prioritise or which direction to take.

Underperforming app experience

  • A dated and fragmented app, particularly in the booking journey
  • Unclear pricing structures leading to confusion and customer service dependency
  • High friction in selecting routes, managing passenger details, and completing bookings

Organisational constraints

  • Complex backend systems handling real-time boarding, check-in, and passenger validation
  • Strong dependencies on external partners
  • A low product maturity organisation working in a highly waterfall-driven way
════════════════════════════════════
My Role

UX Lead & Project Planning Lead. I led both the UX work and the structure of the project, working closely with a UI designer and an external backend partner.

Responsibilities

  • Defined timelines, milestones, and ways of working together with the client's product owner
  • Led research, synthesis, and product direction
  • Facilitated workshops and stakeholder alignment
  • Worked closely with a UI designer and external partners
════════════════════════════════════
Approach

The work was structured across three phases: building understanding, defining direction, and aligning the organisation around a shared plan

01 — Understand users, business, and current experience

  • On-site visits in Ireland, boat tours
  • Strong alignment across teams and stakeholders
  • stakeholder interviews across operations, customer service, marketing, & finance
  • research into different travel behaviours, competitive analysis, and UX evaluations of the current experience

02 — Define product direction and UX foundations

Based on the insights collected, I defined an initial product strategy, creating a shared understanding of what the product should be and how it should evolve.

  • Creation of product and brand values, together with UX principles to guide future product development
  • Personas and key user groups

03 — Align stakeholders and drive decisions

A big part of the project was bringing people together. Through workshops and ongoing collaboration, I aligned stakeholders across different departments, helped teams agree on key problems and priorities, and connected business goals with user needs. This step was key to moving forward, especially in an organisation with many stakeholders and a more traditional way of working.

════════════════════════════════════
Outcome

The definition phase established a clear and shared product direction, turning a complex and unclear situation into a structured plan. The work was well received across the organisation and created strong alignment, allowing the project to move forward into the next phase, which is planned for release under 2026.

What was delivered

  • A clear product direction based on user needs and business goals
  • Strong alignment across teams and stakeholders
  • A solid foundation for redesign, including UX principles, product strategy and a revamped brand

What I did

UX Lead and project planning lead — on-site research, stakeholder workshops, product strategy, UX principles, and persona definition across an 8-week definition phase.

The challenge

A fragmented app, unclear priorities, waterfall-driven organisation, and complex backend dependencies — with no shared understanding of where to start.

The outcome

A clear product direction, strong stakeholder alignment, and a solid UX foundation — ready to move into redesign and development.
