In addition to hands-on UX work, I took on leadership responsibilities in two forms: first as a team lead within the PostNord team, and later as a people manager for designers across the organisation.

Team lead and people manager alongside hands-on UX work: coaching designers, improving team structures, and enabling delivery across teams.

Team Lead

As team lead, I worked closely with both the team and stakeholders to connect roadmap decisions with what was actually happening in the sprint. Because I was still actively designing, I had a strong understanding of the team's day-to-day reality and could help bridge execution, planning, and stakeholder expectations.

Responsibilities

  • Led sprint planning, retrospectives, and stakeholder alignment while working hands-on as a UX designer
  • Improved team ways of working through actionable retrospective follow-ups, meeting changes, and process refinements
People Manager

As a manager, I supported up to seven designers across different teams through coaching, development talks, feedback, and career progression. I also helped improve the structures around development by redesigning the development talk process and initiating work to clarify design role levels and expectations across the organisation.

Responsibilities

  • Coached and mentored designers across teams through weekly one-on-ones and development check-ins
  • Managed development talks, feedback collection, progression discussions, and salary input
  • Redesigned development frameworks and initiated role-level definitions for designers from junior to expert
  • Contributed to design leadership initiatives within the competence area, including learning and skill-building activities
Reflection

Through both roles I developed strong skills in coaching, alignment, and team enablement. At the same time, these experiences clarified that my strongest contribution and motivation lies in hands-on design work. This realisation has made me a stronger designer and collaborator, ready to support with my skill-set wherever it's needed the most.
