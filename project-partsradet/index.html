Michela Monterosso EXPERIENCE ABOUT Unifying & simplifying a key governmental website TL;DR ￼ TYPE App Design CLIENT Client Name YEAR 2025 The Social Partners' Council (Partsrådet) is a non-profit organisation funded by state agencies in cooperation with unions. Their mission is to support social partners in the workplaces for the Swedish government sector, consisting of about 250 agencies and organisations. OVERVIEW As UX lead, I was involved from the earliest strategic conversations all the way through to implementation. Over five months I worked closely with all parts of the organisation — running workshops, interviews, and weekly concept-testing sessions — helping break down internal silos and align teams around a shared vision. The role required full proficiency in Swedish to communicate effectively with both users and internal stakeholders across a politically sensitive government context. 01 — EMPATHISE & DEFINE The project opened with a deep-dive into the organisation: what Partsrådet actually does, what it offers, and how it operates internally. Understanding those dynamics was essential before any design work could begin. User interviews, effect mapping workshops, and a full site audit surfaced the core pain points — a navigation system with clear usability problems, scattered content, and internal language that didn't speak to users. User interviews Effect mapping Site audit Effect mapping workshops and user interview synthesis 02 — IDEATE & STRUCTURE With both user and stakeholder needs mapped, I designed a new sitemap, navigation, and content structure — rethinking information hierarchy and taxonomy from the ground up. Early low-fi wireframes were used to explore and validate ideas quickly. Sitemap IA & taxonomy Low-fi wireframes New sitemap and early low-fidelity wireframes 03 — PROTOTYPE & TEST An iterative approach to wireframing and interaction flows, with regular prototype testing with both clients and real users. Working collaboratively with developers, I also built out the main component library and design system. Wireframes Prototyping Component system Dev collaboration Mid-fidelity wireframes and interaction flows Component library and prototype testing sessions OUTCOME The new site is measurably clearer and easier to use. A refreshed brand identity ties the work together — making offerings, content, and contacts quickly accessible in a way that reflects the organisation's ambition. The experience went from fragmented and hard to navigate to cohesive and intuitive.
